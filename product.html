What is the ARMADAV?

Description

The Project ARMADAV stands for Autonomous Radiation Monitoring and Data-logging Aquatic Vehicle. Its main objective is to record radioactivity data in the oceans. The ARMADAV consists of several components such as the bGeigie Nano, ATX2 Arduino microcontroller, navigation and propulsion systems, in a watertight trimaran drone design. The device is unmanned and does not necessarily require a human controller thus limits the need for manpower in a highly specialized field of nuclear science and environment monitoring.

Specifications

Technologies

  • bGeigie Nano radiation logger
    • LND 7317
    • OpenLogger Data-logging module
    • GPS Sensor
  • ATX2 Microcontroller
  • HMC5883L Digital Compass
  • Water Pumps
  • Solar Panel
  • Lithium-ion batteries
  • Long Range Communication module (optional)

Functions

  • Navigate and maneuver to programmed locations autonomously
  • Record surface radiation along the path it takes
  • Stay afloat despite rough seas
  • Power itself by using solar and battery reserves

Future Plans

ARMADAV Full-auto awaits further trial runs and implementation with the help of nuclear agencies. On the other hand, ARMADAV Semi-auto development is about to begin upon funding and materials are available.
